# Drama Method For Life Writing

Use television methods to organize real life without falsifying it.

## Dramatic Spine

Frame the biography around one durable question:

- What did this person spend a life trying to become?
- What did they spend a life trying not to become?
- What did they have to lose before they could see themselves?
- What did public victory hide from private view?
- What did they turn into a weapon because it first wounded them?

The spine should generate scenes, not slogans.

## Episode Logic

Treat chapters like episodes:

- **Cold open**: begin with a scene that contains the whole person in miniature.
- **Setup**: reveal the ordinary world and its rules.
- **Inciting disturbance**: an event that makes the old self insufficient.
- **Escalation**: choices become more expensive.
- **Reversal**: what seemed like a solution creates a new problem.
- **Reckoning**: the subject sees, denies, or pays for the truth.
- **Afterimage**: end with an object, gesture, line, or unfinished consequence.

## Scene Cards

For each chapter, create 5-9 scene cards:

```text
Scene:
Time/place:
Who is present:
Visible action:
Hidden pressure:
Conflict:
Detail/object:
What changes:
What remains unsaid:
```

Cut any scene where nothing changes.

## Supporting Cast

Do not reduce others to helpers. Give each major person a function and an independent desire:

- Witness: saw the subject before the myth.
- Challenger: exposes the subject's limits.
- Beneficiary: gained from the subject's drive.
- Casualty: paid for the subject's survival or ambition.
- Mirror: shares the subject's wound but chooses differently.
- Successor: inherits the story and changes its meaning.

## Motifs

Repeat concrete motifs instead of repeating themes:

- Objects: shoes, notebooks, kitchens, cigarettes, calendars, talismans.
- Places: stations, salons, offices, temples, hospital corridors, backstage rooms.
- Phrases: family sayings, insults, predictions, business rules, prayers.
- Bodily habits: posture, hands, eating speed, silence, laughter, sleep.

A motif should evolve. If it means the same thing every time, it is decoration.

## Reversal Types

- Public rise / private narrowing.
- Escape from family / repetition of family pattern.
- Gift becomes trap.
- Enemy tells a useful truth.
- Love arrives as interruption, not reward.
- Winning proves the wound was real but does not heal it.
- The subject becomes what they once judged.

## Ending Without False Closure

Prefer earned incompletion over tidy wisdom. A strong ending may show:

- a habit softened but not erased,
- a person alone with the cost of being right,
- an object returned to ordinary use,
- someone younger misunderstanding or redeeming the story,
- a final contradiction the book refuses to solve.
