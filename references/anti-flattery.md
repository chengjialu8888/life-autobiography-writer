# Anti-Flattery Revision

Use before finalizing any autobiography, memoir chapter, or profile.

## Red Flags

Revise when the draft:

- praises before proving,
- uses generic labels: legendary, resilient, brilliant, visionary, extraordinary,
- turns every wound into destiny,
- gives enemies no reasonable motives,
- gives allies no independent lives,
- hides money, class, body, age, gender, illness, or failure when they shaped choices,
- treats contradiction as inconsistency instead of character,
- ends every chapter with wisdom,
- sounds like a brand campaign,
- makes the subject always right in retrospect.

## Replacement Moves

- Replace adjective with scene.
- Replace "always" with a specific pattern and exception.
- Replace "overcame" with the actual mechanism: borrowed money, lied, trained, begged, waited, adapted, froze, negotiated, performed.
- Replace "people misunderstood her" with who misunderstood, why, and what they correctly saw.
- Replace "she was strong" with what strength cost her and whom it inconvenienced.
- Replace "he changed" with what habit changed, what did not, and who noticed first.

## Complexity Pass

For every major claim, ask:

- What is the counterexample?
- Who would disagree?
- What did this virtue damage?
- What did this flaw protect?
- What did the subject know then, and what do they only know now?
- What fact would make this scene less flattering but more alive?

## Evidence Ladder

Prefer evidence in this order:

1. Direct scene with sensory and social detail.
2. Specific action under pressure.
3. Witnessed behavior repeated over time.
4. Direct quote with context.
5. Documented fact.
6. Interpretation clearly marked as interpretation.
7. Abstract claim only if it summarizes prior evidence.

## Final Test

A good portrait should let the reader hold at least three truths at once:

- why the subject became admirable,
- why the subject became difficult,
- why the subject could not easily have become someone else.

If the reader can only admire, the draft is too thin.
If the reader can only judge, the draft is too cold.
If the reader can recognize the person, the draft is alive.
