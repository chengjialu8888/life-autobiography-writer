# Question Bank

Use these questions in rounds. Do not dump the whole list at once. Pick 8-12 questions, then follow the heat.

## Assignment Calibration

- Who is this for, and what should the reader feel after reading?
- Should the piece protect, reveal, reconcile, provoke, preserve, or understand?
- What version of this person already exists in public or family memory?
- What version would be dangerous, embarrassing, or freeing to tell?
- What must not be touched without explicit permission?

## Chronology With Pressure

- What is the origin point of this life?
- If one hunger drove the life forward, what was it?
- What is the first scene that explains something about this person?
- What is the first childhood scene that still feels charged?
- What did the family reward? What did it punish?
- What kind of child did adults think they were? What did adults miss?
- When did they first learn money, status, beauty, gender, class, or power mattered?
- What was the first decision they made against expectation?
- What did they leave too early? What did they leave too late?
- What did success make possible, and what did it make impossible?

## Scene Excavation

- Where were they sitting or standing when the decision happened?
- What was the room like? Who else was there?
- What object from that period could still tell the truth?
- What sentence did someone say that they never forgot?
- What did they do immediately after the event?
- What did their body remember before their mind understood?
- What would a camera see that the official story would omit?

## Contradiction

- What do people admire about them that also causes trouble?
- What do they say they value, and where do their actions complicate that?
- When are they most generous? When are they most controlling?
- What accusation against them contains a grain of truth?
- What praise of them is secretly incomplete?
- What do they still perform, even when no one is watching?

## Relationships

- Who saw them most clearly?
- Who benefited from their ambition? Who paid for it?
- Who did they underestimate?
- Who did they need but resent needing?
- Who did they hurt while trying to survive?
- Who changed the story by refusing to play their assigned role?

## Work And Craft

- What did they practice when nobody cared?
- What did they learn by failing publicly?
- What skill looks like instinct but was actually built?
- What rule did they break because the old rule did not fit reality?
- What did they become known for that did not feel like the whole self?

## Shame, Hunger, And Repair

- What did they once want so badly that it frightened them?
- What kind of humiliation shaped their taste?
- What did they mistake for love, safety, or victory?
- What apology would matter, even if it is never spoken?
- What do they forgive in others because they recognize it in themselves?
- What do they refuse to forgive because it kept them alive?

## Counter-Narrative

- If an enemy wrote this life, what would they emphasize?
- If a child wrote it, what would they misunderstand?
- If an old friend wrote it, what would be warmer and less polished?
- What would the subject deny first?
- Which chapter would make the subject angry but not because it is false?

## Ending Questions

- What has time made smaller?
- What has time made sharper?
- What remains unresolved, and why should it remain unresolved?
- What should the last image be: an object, gesture, room, sentence, or unfinished task?
